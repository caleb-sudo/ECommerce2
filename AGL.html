An astronaut's guide to life
$10.00

By: Chris Hadfield

As Commander of the International Space Station, Chris Hadfield captured our imagination. In his #1 internationally bestselling An Astronaut's Guide to Life on Earth, he reveals how his impossible dream of becoming an astronaut came true.

When he was nine, Chris Hadfield watched the Apollo moon landing and knew what he wanted to be when he grew up: an astronaut. But Canadians couldn't even apply for the job. So how did he go on to become the first Canadian Commander of the International Space Station in 2013? Natural ability didn't hurt. But even more important was his commitment to "thinking like an astronaut." Astronaut training turns conventional wisdom about success on its head. Astronauts always sweat the small stuff and they embrace the power of negative thinking-and yet the outcomes, both professionally and personally, are resoundingly positive. Hadfield illustrates these counterintuitive concepts with dramatic and entertaining anecdotes about being blinded during a spacewalk, getting rid of a live snake while flying a plane, and commanding a space ship while its lifeblood leaks into space. An Astronaut's Guide to Life on Earth isn't just the inspiring story of one man's journey to the ISS. It's an opportunity to step into his space-boots and learn to think like an astronaut-and figure out how to make your own dreams come true.
